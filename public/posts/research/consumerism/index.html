🌱 Consumerism

Jul 02, 2026 | 2 min read

Objective

Understand the psychology and cultural systems behind why we buy and what sustains overconsumption, with particular focus on how information overwhelm prevents consumers from translating awareness into meaningful action. Connects to SDG 12 as a structural sustainability lens.

Why this topic

I'm interested in the gap between knowing and doing, specifically how the volume and inaccessibility of supply chain information makes ethical consumption structurally difficult, not just personally inconvenient.

Working thesis

Informed consumers still cannot act meaningfully on what they know. The barrier is not awareness but cognitive capacity: the volume of information required to make genuinely ethical purchasing decisions exceeds what any individual can process. Overconsumption is therefore not primarily a moral failure but a systems design problem.

Current stage

Early. Brief exposure to SDG 12, no deep reading yet.

Key tensions

Framing overconsumption as a systems problem risks removing individual accountability entirely. The awareness-to-action gap is real but some consumers do change behaviour — what separates them? Is the solution better information design, or does meaningful change require structural intervention at the supply or policy level?

Open questions

  • What shifts a consumer from awareness to action?
  • Where is the line for what counts as overconsumption?
  • What is SDG 12 in practice beyond the principle?
  • What are the historical and structural factors that got us here?
