🌱 Hyperreality

Jul 02, 2026 | 1 min read

Objective

Understand Baudrillard's concept of hyperreality and how it maps onto contemporary digital life — specifically how algorithmically mediated reality shapes how people understand the world, form identity, and respond to events.

Why this topic

The idea that a thinker predicted the exact conditions we now live in is striking. Particularly interested in how algorithmic bias creates a filtered version of reality that people treat as complete — and what that means for individual worldview and collective response.

Working thesis

Pending — still orienting to the concept.

Key tensions

Hyperreality assumes people can't distinguish simulation from reality — but some can, and still participate anyway. Is the problem one of awareness, or is participation in the simulation rational even when you know it's happening?

Open questions

  • How does living in an algorithmically filtered reality affect mental health?
  • How does it shape identity formation, particularly for people who grew up with social media?
  • What does it mean for individual agency if your understanding of reality is itself a curated output?
