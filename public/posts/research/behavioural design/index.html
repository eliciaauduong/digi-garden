🌱 Behavioural design

Jul 02, 2026 | 2 min read

Objective

Understand how environments — physical and digital — shape human behaviour, with particular focus on what makes spaces and platforms foster connection, return, and positive emotional states. Draw from third space theory and community design as a countermodel to extractive platform design.

Why this topic

Fascination with how physical spaces naturally cultivate community and belonging, and curiosity about whether those principles can translate digitally. Underlying belief that human capacity for change, empathy, and connection is underutilised by platforms that optimise for negative engagement instead.

Working thesis

Behavioural design can reinforce positive human behaviour, but good intent does not resolve the ethics. The line between inspiration and manipulation does not move based on the designer's purpose — which means even well-meaning design raises unresolved questions about consent and agency. The fact that it could be used for good does not make it not manipulation.

Current stage

Fresh. No prior reading in behavioural design or psychology.

Key tensions

Designing for positive behaviour still involves shaping behaviour without full user awareness — the mechanism is the same as coercive design, only the intent differs. If manipulation is defined by method rather than outcome, positive behavioural design is still manipulation. If defined by outcome, who decides what counts as positive?

Open questions

  • What makes physical third spaces generate genuine community and return visits — and are those principles transferable to digital?
  • How can platforms be designed to reinforce hope, care, and respect rather than rage and anxiety?
  • What will it take to get meaningful user consent for positive reinforcement design?
  • Does giving permission help or hinder agency?
