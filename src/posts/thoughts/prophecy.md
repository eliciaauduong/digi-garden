## Objective
Understand how trends form, spread, and fade — and whether platforms identify cultural momentum or manufacture it. Examine the relationship between trends and purchasing behaviour, overconsumption, and long-term value, and whether trends are becoming less meaningful as amplification mechanisms become more visible.

## Why this topic
Direct professional exposure to trend prediction at Pinterest and on the Accenture Song research team — identifying signals for emerging trends across different years. That experience raised a genuine uncertainty: when trends are predicted and published, does that accelerate their adoption? Is the prediction the trend?

## Working thesis
Trends are increasingly self-fulfilling. When platforms predict and amplify cultural signals, they don't just identify momentum — they create it. The act of naming a trend redistributes attention toward it, accelerating adoption and validating the prediction. Manufacture and identification become indistinguishable. But even genuine trends produce limited long-term value — they generate engagement and drive overconsumption without creating lasting cultural meaning or customer value. Trends may be losing their power precisely because the machinery behind them is becoming visible.

## Current stage
Some professional exposure through trend research. No formal reading on trend theory.

## Key tensions
If trends are self-fulfilling, the ethical question for trend researchers is whether publishing predictions is neutral observation or active intervention. Also: not all trends are algorithmically manufactured — some emerge from genuine grassroots cultural shifts. How do you distinguish organic momentum from amplified signal, and does it matter if the outcome is the same?

## Open questions
- Are trends actually popular or is algorithmic amplification creating the appearance of popularity? 
- How much do trends measurably influence purchasing and consumer behaviour? 
- How does trend culture contribute to overconsumption? 
- Will trends always hold cultural power or is visibility into the machinery eroding their influence? 
- What is the long-term value — if any — that trends deliver to consumers?
